A devastating mountain landslide that struck the village of Malpa

üìå Overview

The Malpa landslide of 1998 impacted the small Himalayan settlement of Malpa in the Pithoragarh region. Steep slopes, intense monsoon rainfall and fragile geology combined to produce a catastrophic slope failure that destroyed homes and infrastructure in the valley.

  • Date: 1998
  • Location: Malpa, Pithoragarh district (Uttarakhand)
  • Impact: Village heavily damaged; houses and terraces lost
  • Weather: Prolonged heavy monsoon rains preceding the collapse
  • Primary causes: Slope instability, altered drainage, deforestation

  • Intense and sustained monsoon rainfall saturating the slope
  • Fragile geology and deeply weathered rock layers
  • Steep agricultural terraces and slope modification
  • Poor or altered drainage increasing pore-water pressures
  • Vegetation removal and local land-use changes

  • Terraced fields ‚Äì agricultural land lost or buried

üìö Lessons Learned
