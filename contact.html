Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I live in a high-risk landslide area?

If you live in a high-risk area, take these steps: (1) Create an evacuation plan, (2) Prepare an emergency kit, (3) Stay informed about weather alerts, (4) Know the warning signs of landslides, (5) Register with local disaster management authorities, (6) Participate in community preparedness programs.

How can I report a landslide hazard?

You can report landslide hazards through: (1) NDMA Emergency: 011-26701728, (2) National Disaster Helpline: 1078, (3) Your local District Disaster Management Authority, (4) Contact your local block development officer or panchayat, (5) Use this website's contact form to report concerns.

What are the main warning signs of an impending landslide?

Warning signs include: cracks in ground or buildings, tilting trees, water seeping from slopes, rocks or soil falling from hillsides, loud ground noises, ground bulging, and unusual animal behavior. If you notice these signs, evacuate immediately and contact authorities.

How often do landslides occur in India?

India experiences 500-1000 landslides annually, mostly during the monsoon season (June-September). The frequency has increased in recent years due to climate change, deforestation, and unplanned development. High-risk states like Uttarakhand, Kerala, and Himachal Pradesh experience the most incidents.

What should be in an emergency kit?

Essential items: water (1 liter per person daily, 3-day supply), non-perishable food, first aid kit, medications, flashlight, batteries, portable radio, important documents, cash, mobile charger, comfortable shoes, and protective gear (helmet, gloves). Keep it easily accessible.
