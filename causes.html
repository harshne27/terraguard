Causes and Effects

Understanding What Triggers Landslides and Their Impacts

Home / Causes & Effects

Natural Causes

  • Heavy Rainfall: Most common trigger; saturation reduces soil strength
  • Earthquakes: Seismic vibrations can destabilize slopes
  • Weak Rock Formations: Poor geological structures prone to failure
  • River Erosion: Undercutting of slopes by water

Anthropogenic Causes

  • Deforestation: Loss of vegetation reduces slope stability
  • Unplanned Construction: Poor building practices on slopes
  • Road Cutting: Destabilizes slope geometry
  • Mining Activities: Alters slope structure and stability

Effects of Landslides

üë• Human Impact & Casualties

Landslides cause direct loss of life through immediate impact and burial under debris. Survivors face injuries and trauma. Displacement from affected areas disrupts lives and livelihoods. The 2024 Wayanad disaster resulted in 400+ deaths with hundreds still missing.

üè¢ Infrastructure Damage

Roads, bridges, and railways are destroyed or become impassable. Communication networks are disrupted. Water and electricity supply systems are damaged. Buildings are destroyed or rendered uninhabitable. Recovery of infrastructure requires significant time and resources.

üí∞ Economic Losses

Property damage runs into billions of rupees annually. Agricultural land is destroyed, affecting farmer income. Tourism and commercial activities cease in affected areas. Government spending on relief, rescue, and reconstruction strains budgets.

üåç Environmental Impact

Soil erosion accelerates in affected regions. Forest cover is destroyed, causing long-term ecological damage. Rivers are blocked by debris, altering water flow and aquatic ecosystems. Biodiversity is reduced. Environmental recovery takes years or decades.
