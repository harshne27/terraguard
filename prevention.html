Four Pillars of Landslide Risk Management

üèóÔ∏è Engineering Solutions

Structural measures to stabilize slopes and prevent movement.

  • Retaining Walls: Concrete or gabion walls to hold back soil
  • Rock Bolts: Anchoring unstable rock masses
  • Drainage Systems: Reducing water-induced instability
  • Slope Terracing: Breaking long slopes into shorter segments
  • Shotcrete: Reinforced concrete sprayed on slopes

üå± Biological Methods

Natural approaches using vegetation to stabilize slopes.

  • Afforestation: Planting trees on vulnerable slopes
  • Grass/Shrub Planting: Quick stabilization with vegetation
  • Root Reinforcement: Using deep-rooted plants for stability
  • Bioengineering: Combining vegetation with structural elements

üö® Early Warning Systems

Technology and monitoring to predict and prepare for landslides.

  • Real-Time Monitoring: GPS and tilt sensors tracking slope movement
  • Rainfall Tracking: Identifying dangerous rainfall patterns
  • LiDAR Monitoring: Detecting subtle elevation changes
  • Satellite Monitoring: NRSC using remote sensing
  • Alert Systems: SMS and siren-based warnings

üìã Land Use Planning

Regulatory and planning approaches to reduce risk.

  • Hazard Zonation: Mapping high-risk areas
  • Building Codes: Enforcing safe construction standards
  • Development Restrictions: Preventing construction in high-risk zones
  • Relocation Programs: Moving people from danger zones
  • Community Awareness: Education and preparedness

NDMA Guidelines & Recommendations

National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) Strategies

  • Landslide Hazard Zonation Mapping: Systematic identification of vulnerable areas
  • Early Warning System Development: Community-based monitoring networks
  • Structural Mitigation: Prioritized intervention in high-risk areas
  • Non-Structural Measures: Regulation, awareness, and preparedness
  • Land Use Planning: Preventing unsafe development in susceptible zones
  • Community Capacity Building: Training programs and education
  • Coordination with Agencies: GSI, NRSC, State governments
