Concise case study of the Pettimudi slope failure event (Kerala) for awareness and planning.

üìå Overview

The Pettimudi landslide (2020) occurred in a steep, high-rainfall section of the Western Ghats (Kerala). Heavy monsoon rains and local slope conditions produced slope failure with impacts to settlements and access routes.

  • Date: 2020 (monsoon period)
  • Region: Pettimudi area, Western Ghats, Kerala
  • Nature: Rainfall-triggered slope failure on steep terrain
  • Primary concerns: loss of vegetation, road damage, isolated communities

üåßÔ∏è Contributing factors

  • Prolonged and intense monsoon rainfall saturating soils
  • Steep slopes with weathered rock and lateritic soils
  • Land-use changes (terracing, plantation clearing, slope cutting)
  • Poor surface drainage and blocked natural channels
  • Reduced root reinforcement after vegetation removal

üìç Affected/local impact

  • Roads and access routes blocked or damaged, impeding relief
  • Damage to a small number of structures and agricultural terraces
  • Local displacement and temporary shelter needs for affected families
  • Increased downstream sedimentation and loss of vegetation cover

üß≠ Timeline (condensed)

  1. Pre-event: Extended heavy monsoon rainfall raises groundwater levels
  2. Trigger: Intense rain episode causes slope saturation and failure
  3. Immediate: Access disruption, local rescue and initial damage assessment
  4. Response: Local authorities coordinate relief, clear debris, and assess long-term risks

üõ†Ô∏è Response & lessons

  • Immediate search and rescue where required and restoration of access routes
  • Short-term slope stabilization and drainage clearance
  • Enforceable land-use controls and safer construction practices on steep slopes
  • Install early-warning triggers (rain gauges, simple pore-pressure/rainfall thresholds)
  • Community awareness, evacuation planning and routine hazard mapping
