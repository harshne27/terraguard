üö® BEFORE A LANDSLIDE

Prepare and Reduce Risk

‚ö†Ô∏è Know the Warning Signs

  • Cracks on the ground or in buildings
  • Tilting or falling trees
  • Water seeping from slopes
  • Rocks or soil falling from hillsides
  • Loud noises from the ground
  • Ground bulging or subsiding

üìç Create an Evacuation Plan

  • Identify multiple evacuation routes from your home and workplace
  • Mark high ground areas where you can take shelter
  • Plan meeting points for your family
  • Practice evacuation drills regularly
  • Ensure elderly and children know the plan

üéí Prepare an Emergency Kit

Essential items to keep ready:

  • Drinking water (1 liter per person per day, 3-day supply)
  • Non-perishable food for 3 days
  • First aid kit and medications
  • Flashlights and extra batteries
  • Portable radio for emergency alerts
  • Important documents (copies)
  • Cash and credit cards
  • Mobile phone charger and power bank
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Protective gear (helmet, gloves)

üå™Ô∏è DURING A LANDSLIDE

Immediate Actions to Stay Safe

‚ö° If Outdoors

  • Move perpendicular to the slide direction: Run sideways away from the slide path
  • Reach high ground: Climb to higher elevations away from the path
  • Protect your head: Use your arms or hands to shield your head
  • Avoid river channels: They can become flow paths for debris
  • Do not use bridges: They may be destroyed or unstable
  • Stay away from trees and boulders: They can be carried by the slide

üè† If Indoors

  • Evacuate immediately if ordered: Do not wait for official confirmation
  • Move to higher floors: Go upstairs and to the back of the building
  • Avoid ground floors: Higher positions are safer
  • Close windows and doors: Helps prevent debris entry
  • If trapped: Stay in place and signal for help
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts: Follow official instructions

‚úÖ AFTER A LANDSLIDE

Recovery and Safety Measures

üö® Immediate Safety Checks

  • Check for injuries: Provide first aid and call for medical help
  • Inspect your home: Look for structural damage, gas leaks, electrical damage
  • Turn off utilities if damaged: Gas, electricity, and water may be hazardous
  • Do not enter damaged buildings: They may collapse
  • Listen for afterslides: More landslides may follow heavy rain

ü§ù Helping Others

  • Check on neighbors and elderly: Ensure their safety
  • Provide first aid: If trained, help injured people
  • Report missing people: Contact police and rescue authorities immediately
  • Participate in community cleanup: Help remove debris safely
  • Support affected families: Provide food, shelter, emotional support
